Charlie Munger, this is the man Warren Buffet's mate, says the human brain is a bit like the human egg, that once a sperm enters the egg, it closes down and prevents anything entering. Once a first decision, a first impression or prejudice enters our brain, we close down to further stimuli. If you checked in within three minutes and had a good check-in experience, your every other perception of that hotel tended to be good. Because you made a decision, this is a good hotel, I'm going to like it, and you looked for affirmatory evidence. If you had to queue 15 or 20 minutes to check in, everything about the hotel was bad. Not just perception of the service, which might be reasonably coloured by that, but actually perception of things like the ice sculpture in the garden. People actually ticking satisfaction questionnaires, if they'd had a 20-minute wait for check-in, were very keen to say that they thought the ice sculpture was shit.
