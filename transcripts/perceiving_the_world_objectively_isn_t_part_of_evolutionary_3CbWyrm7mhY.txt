It's not our evolutionary interests to perceive the world objectively. It's in our evolutionary interest to perceive the world in a way that's useful and that allows us to make quick, safe, non-catastrophic decisions. The non-catastrophic parts are really important one because when we talk in marketing, we always talk about what's good about a brand. The instinctive system one brain is overwhelmingly preoccupied with what might be terrible. And we never talk about that because we like words like sunny, bright, contemporary. The reason McDonald's is the most successful restaurant in the world isn't because it's fantastically good, it's because it's incredibly good at not being shit. You are less likely to get ill at McDonald's than at a Michelin three-star restaurant. You won't get ripped off. You won't be disappointed because the food is exactly the same. The absence of negatives is really important to us. We just never talk about it because, again, it's so important it's instinctive.
