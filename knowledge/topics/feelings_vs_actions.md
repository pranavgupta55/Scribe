# Feelings vs Actions

> Feelings are real but don't mandate behavior; create space between feeling and acting.

## Key Takeaway
- Beginners must separate feeling from action
- Feel hopeless but continue goal-aligned activities anyway
- Feel hungry but don't eat the cookie

## Framework
- Create gap between stimulus (feeling) and response (action)
- Gap = time = more logical decision-making possible

## Claims
- Feelings don't require action; creating space (time) between feeling and acting allows logical decisions instead of reactive ones — [a_lifetime_of_personal_development_in_18_minutes_PsxOnxIU274.txt]

## Connections
- Breaking the link between emotion and behavior is core maturity; act normally despite feelings. _(connects to: Separation Of Feeling And Action)_
- Confidence follows action, not vice versa; separate feeling from behavior to execute despite doubt. _(connects to: Action Decoupled From Feeling)_
- Both show that identity comes from observable actions, not feelings; you are what you do, and space between feeling and acting determines behavior. _(connects to: Evidence Over Emotion)_
