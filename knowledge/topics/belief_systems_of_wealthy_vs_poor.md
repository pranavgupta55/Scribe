# Belief Systems of Wealthy vs. Poor

> Wealthy parents teach children high-leverage career paths exist; poor people don't know these paths or that high pricing is possible; wealth mindsets differ fundamentally.

## Key Takeaway
- Rich kids choose not to pursue low-leverage paths because they were taught it wasn't worth time
- Poor people don't know about management consulting, private equity, investment banking (founder only knew 'doctor' as 'rich')
- Wealthy people will make you one of them if you sell to them long enough

## Why It Works
- Network access, beliefs, and thinking patterns gradually shift when immersed in wealthy customer base
- Rich customers operate on ROI and value logic; poor customers operate on cost logic—fundamentally different evaluation

## Examples
- Coming from Baltimore, founder thought 'rich' was owning business with 2 secretaries; at Vanderbilt saw New York/California money for first time—completely different scale
- Father had immigrant mentality (don't waste paper towels); founder carried this even while building $250M portfolio—mindset lags wealth growth
