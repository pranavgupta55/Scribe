# Content Format & Production

> Emphasize language over effects; clear visuals over razzle-dazzle; pre-production over post-production; vague-to-clear thumbnails and proof-based intros.

## Key Numbers
- 75% of top videos emphasized language/message; 75% of bottom videos had production effects emphasis
- Top long-form videos: 70-80% words, minimal effects
- Pre-production to post-production time ratio: spending 25% on pre saves 90-95% of post work

## Five Tactical Changes
- Packaging: vague thumbnails + headlines → clear thumbnails + headlines (tell exactly what it's about)
- Intros: Proof-Promise-Plan framework (credibility, value promise, roadmap)
- Meat: story/steps over vlogs/be-real content (language over razzle-dazzle)
- Visuals: data visualization over effects (charts showing scale over flames/pop-ups)
- Pre-work: spend time researching and outlining before filming; eliminate 90%+ of editing work

## Why It Works
- Clarity increases perceived likelihood that viewer will get what they clicked for
- Educational viewers need clarity to learn; production effects distract from learning
- Pre-production planning ensures efficient filming and editing
