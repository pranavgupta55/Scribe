# Capacity Expansion

> Each limit you break raises the ceiling for what you believe you can handle.

## Framework
- Face new challenge → survive it → update mental model of capacity
- Repeat → ceiling keeps rising

## Implications
- The hardest you've ever worked becomes the new baseline, not the maximum

## Claims
- Every new challenge shows you a new territory you were scared of but survived; every time you break a limit, you know you have more capacity than before. — [you_re_not_winning_you_re_just_getting_used_to_pain_IxqZEmO9WUQ.txt]

## Connections
- Building capacity enables recognizing opportunities others miss; breaking limits raises ceiling. _(connects to: Capacity Building)_
- Pushing your capacity raises your ceiling for what you believe you can handle. _(connects to: Effort Scaling)_
- Work capacity grows with volume exposure; burnout signals capacity limit, not breaking point requiring lifestyle change. _(connects to: Work Capacity And Adaptation)_
