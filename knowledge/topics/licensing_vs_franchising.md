# Licensing vs Franchising

> Franchising doesn't fit low-margin businesses; licensing at 6-8% top line is better, but only after proving team scalability.

## Framework
- Franchising: requires capital component; you get ~30% of upside, licensee gets 70%; high overhead
- Licensing: 6-8% top line, licensee keeps rest, you keep your margins

## Key Numbers
- Licensing at 6-8% of $500K = $30K-$40K per location monthly
- But only do it once you can open 1 location/quarter without founder

## Caveats
- Insurance licensure patchwork: 700 companies, varying state rules = licensing complexity

- Franchising requires capital intensity as justification; a $500K per-location, $75K per-location profit business doesn't justify franchise overhead; licensing at 6-8% top line is better. — [how_to_say_no_to_the_wrong_opportunities_YJy7PL2apUo.txt]
