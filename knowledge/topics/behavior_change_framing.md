# Behavior-Change Framing

> Position offer as behavior change (stop doing X, start doing Y), not emotional state (centeredness).

## Framework
- Current outcome: centeredness, removing patterns, breaking childhood trauma cycles
- Reframe: behavior change that removes unwanted patterns and enables desired behaviors

## Why It Works
- Creates accountability on client to change behavior
- Creates accountability on coach to deliver behavior change, not just mindset shift

## Claims
- Current offer language (centeredness, removing trauma) is ethereal and confuses audience; reframe as behavior change. — [my_spiritual_coaching_offer_isn_t_converting_help_qsGvub8nxaU.txt]

## Connections
- Position offers as behavior change (stop/start actions), not emotional states; aversive stimulus creates permanent shifts. _(connects to: Trauma Permanent Behavior Change)_
- Positioning offers as behavior change (actions) rather than emotional state (identity) removes friction and enables adoption. _(connects to: Behavioral Flexibility)_
- Frame offers as behavior change not emotion; trauma requires documented behavioral change. _(connects to: Behavior Change Requirement)_
