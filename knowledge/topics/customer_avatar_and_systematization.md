# Customer Avatar and Systematization

> Service businesses must narrow focus from any customer to ideal customer profile; enables systematization and margin expansion

## Framework
- Stage 1 (pre-$1M-3M): service businesses serve any customer with a pulse to generate initial revenue
- Stage 2 ($1M-$3M): decision point to identify which customers are great vs. which suck; choose ideal customer profile (ICP)
- Stage 3 ($3M+): narrow to 1 avatar with 1-3 core deliverables; enables systemization, training, and margin improvement

## How-To
- Identify patterns: which customers stick longest, require less management, create referrals, can afford premium pricing
- Define 1-3 core deliverables for chosen avatar instead of customizing everything; enables repeatable process and training
- Systematize delivery with clear steps so training can be built around processes, not founder expertise
- Use systematization to create career paths and scale headcount without proportional founder involvement increase

## Examples
- Fitness coaching: some gyms sticky and profitable, others high-churn; narrowing to ideal customer profile enables operational leverage
- Management consulting: targeting mid-market companies with recurring operational issues vs. one-off enterprise projects; former enables systematization
- Service specialization: 'We solve X problem in 3 ways: Y, Z, or Q' is more scalable than 'Whatever you want, your wildest dreams'
