# Value Proposition Design

> Reframe pitch from unbelievable savings ($200k) to believable margin arbitrage (20% to 0%)

## Common Mistakes
- $200k savings claim sounds like a scam and causes immediate objections
- Need credibility bridge: show how nonprofit model works vs profit-driven industry

## How-To / Method
- Position as: 'We take the 20% profit margin the industry takes and give it to you'
- 100-employee company pays $2,000 per employee annually via tax code optimization

## Claims
- Nonprofit model takes 20% of savings generated for clients instead, realigning incentives — [why_warren_buffett_calls_this_industry_a_scam_K9YN5sbwR94.txt]
- Company can save a 100-person employer approximately $200,000 ($2,000 per employee) annually using US tax codes — [why_warren_buffett_calls_this_industry_a_scam_K9YN5sbwR94.txt]
- Better value proposition: 'We take the 20% industry profit margin and make it zero for you' — [why_warren_buffett_calls_this_industry_a_scam_K9YN5sbwR94.txt]

## Connections
- Reframe savings from unbelievable ($200k) to credible margin arbitrage; messaging changes perception of value. _(connects to: Value Proposition Repositioning)_
