# Livestock Branding History

> Earliest branding: ranchers burned symbols into cattle to indicate ownership and change behavior.

## Framework
- Branded cow = recognizable ownership, triggers behavior change in finder
- Wild cow = no association, triggers different behavior (eat it or ignore it)

## Implication
- Even unrecognized brand is treated differently than no brand
- Logo = signal of ownership/association

- Historic livestock branding: ranchers burned symbols into cattle; a branded cow belonging to your neighbor was treated differently than a wild cow, proving brand affects behavior — [what_branding_actually_is_and_how_to_build_one_fast_Vt4D1qgo9ic.txt]
- An unrecognized brand (logo on a cow with unknown ownership) still changes behavior more than no brand because people know it belongs to someone — [what_branding_actually_is_and_how_to_build_one_fast_Vt4D1qgo9ic.txt]
