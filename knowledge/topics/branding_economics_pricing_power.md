# Branding Economics & Pricing Power

> Strong brands command premium pricing, lower acquisition costs, and higher advertising ROI—good branding directly translates to profit.

## Key Numbers
- $5 unbranded t-shirt → $60 Nike t-shirt (12x price increase)
- Nike pairing with sports champions increases click-through rates 6x over generic products
- Good branding drives 10x+ pricing power without losing customers to competitors

## How It Works
- Strong brand = higher pricing power (customers accept premium prices)
- Strong brand = lower acquisition costs (more people seek you out)
- Strong brand = better advertising response rates (higher CTR on ads)
- Strong brand = customer loyalty (repeat purchases and lifetime value increase)

## Framework
- Warren Buffett's pricing power test: if you need a prayer session to raise prices 10%, you have a terrible business
- Premium brand positioning requires product quality to match; holes in the shirt undermine the brand promise
- Brand protection is critical: takes 20 years to build, 5 minutes to ruin
