# Pricing Strategy and Tiered Offers

> Price in 5-10X jumps per tier; each new tier should represent 20% of prior tier volume and double total revenue; four-tier model captures entire market.

## Framework
- Tier 1 (Base): $10/mo, 800 customers
- Tier 2: 5-10X higher ($50-100/mo), 20% of Tier 1 volume (160-200 customers)
- Tier 3: 5-10X higher again ($500-1000/mo), 20% of Tier 2 (32-40 customers)
- Tier 4: 5-10X higher ($2500-10000/mo), 20% of Tier 3 (6-8 customers)

## How-To / Method
- Each tier should double total revenue compared to tier below; otherwise not worth operational complexity
- Start from top tier if possible (brand narrative stronger); bottom-up positioning is weak
- Don't misjudge tier gaps: $1K → $1.5K is same tier; $1K → $5-10K is next tier

## Key Numbers
- Impulse purchase (consumer): $500-600
- Higher-ticket service: $3K-10K
- Business owner mid-tier service: $2K-3K/month
- Business owner cheap service: $400-800/month
- Acquisition.com advisory pricing: $100/mo (hobby) → $5K/mo (L1) → $35K/mo (L2) → $135K/mo (L3)
