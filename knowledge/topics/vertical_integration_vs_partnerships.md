# Vertical Integration vs. Partnerships

> E-commerce reliant on partners (3PLs, manufacturers, distributors); perfect model owns manufacturing but partners on logistics

## Key Takeaway
- E-commerce rarely fully vertically integrated; requires partnerships with manufacturers, 3PLs, raw material suppliers, distribution
- Risk: if partner fails, business fails; redundancy required at each level (multiple manufacturers, multiple 3PLs, multiple distributors)
- Scaling requires switching partners: as volume exceeds 3PL capacity (e.g., shipping 10k orders/day), must add redundant 3PLs in different states

## Examples
- Book launch: needed multiple 3PLs across different states to ship millions of books in 72 hours; single 3PL could not handle volume
- Prestige Labs: owned brick-and-mortar gyms, sold online, used distributors, ran ads; relied on network of partners not owned assets
- Perfect e-commerce company: owns manufacturing (cost control, proprietary recipes/techniques) but partners on decentralized logistics by market

## Implications
- Build redundancy at each critical business stage; single point of failure (one manufacturer, one 3PL, one distributor) is dangerous
- Decentralized manufacturing in key markets reduces shipping costs and cycles compared to centralized production
- Own manufacturing if you have trade secrets or unique techniques; otherwise outsourcing to reliable partners is acceptable
