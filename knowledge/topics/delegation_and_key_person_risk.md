# Delegation and Key Person Risk

> Key-man risk (founder involved in every decision) blocks growth; time studies reveal which tasks compound business value vs. which you should delegate.

## Key Takeaway
- Key-man risk is the #1 risk of every business: no one wants to buy it, and the founder will burn out ("kill the business yourself")
- Premium customers create ego-driven resistance to delegation; founder believes only they can manage high-end client relationships (e.g., Yellowstone Club, Zuckerberg projects)

## How-To / Method
- Conduct weekly time studies: set alarm every 15 minutes, log activities, rank by revenue generation to identify high-leverage vs. low-leverage work
- Bottom half of time-log activities should fit into one person's bandwidth (hire new person or redeploy existing staff with bandwidth)
- Once you recover half your time, validate if you can double the business; if yes, delegation is the unlock

## Examples
- Design work and client relationship management may be most valuable (leverage = highest revenue per hour); using your hands is lowest leverage
