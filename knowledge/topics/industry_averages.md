# Industry Averages

> Ignore industry averages; winners operate outside the frame of 'good enough,' playing to win.

## Why Averages Mislead
- Average American: in debt, divorced, overweight, 'mid'
- Using average as benchmark = accepting you won't improve
- Winner in every category beats the average; they don't follow it

## Examples
- Complex machinery: industry = 1-10% net margin; operator achieves 70% net by selling know-how, not commodity
- Amazon margin: ~4% conversion vs 1-2% typical website (trusted platform effect)

## Action
- Reject frames: "this is industry standard, so it's good"
- Ask: what is physically possible? Business is just inputs → higher-value outputs
- If others do 70% margin in your industry, you can too—it's possible
