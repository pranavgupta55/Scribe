# Pairing Products With Customer Values

> Successful brands pair with things audiences already value; failed pairings (like Bud Light + Dylan Mulvaney) lose revenue and damage brand equity.

## Framework
- Identify what your ideal customer likes and values
- Pair your business/product with people, experiences, causes that embody those values
- Avoid pairing with things your ideal audience dislikes
- Curate like a garden: grow good flowers (valued pairings) and pull out weeds (disliked associations)

## Examples
- Nike + LeBron/Tiger (winning, competition) attracts sports enthusiasts; Dolce & Gabbana + Kim Kardashian attracts people valuing fame/luxury
- Bud Light + Dylan Mulvaney: bad for conservative male audience; Bud Light + Shane Gillis + UFC: good for that audience, sales recovered
- Kanye West: overcame bad pairings (controversial statements) by overwhelming with good pairings (new products, albums); people forgot the bad and bought again

## Common Mistakes
- Don't pair with random/distant things just for novelty
- Don't lose your core audience's trust through misaligned pairings
- Don't expect one new pairing to not lose any audience (5 negative reactions expected per 500 positive ones)
