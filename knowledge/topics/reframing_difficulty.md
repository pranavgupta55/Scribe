# Reframing Difficulty

> Anchor yourself to past survival when facing new challenges.

## Example
- Father's frame before fraternity hazing: "Nothing worse than what you've already survived"; made the experience manageable and almost comical

## Why It Works
- Shifts focus from fear of the unknown to evidence of past capacity

## Claims
- When training partners tell you "you're fine" during hard sets, it's a reframe from "keep going, you got this" to "you will survive this, you are not in danger." — [you_re_not_winning_you_re_just_getting_used_to_pain_IxqZEmO9WUQ.txt]
- Before joining a fraternity known for heavy hazing, the speaker's father said: "There is nothing they can do to you that you have not already had worse happen." This frame made the experience manageable. — [you_re_not_winning_you_re_just_getting_used_to_pain_IxqZEmO9WUQ.txt]
