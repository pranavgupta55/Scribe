# The Money Belief Gap

> Beliefs about money held by rich people (some opportunities aren't worth time) create different career paths and life outcomes than poor-person beliefs.

## Key Takeaway
- Rich kids are taught certain opportunities aren't worth their time; poor kids don't have that filter
- This belief difference creates diverging career paths: rich kid avoids low-leverage, poor kid takes anything
- In Baltimore, 'rich' was a doctor; at Vanderbilt, the speaker saw NY and California money—completely different caliber

## Implications
- Knowledge of opportunities mattering more than knowledge of how-to
- Management consulting, PE, IB don't exist in poor neighborhoods; you can't want what you don't know about
- Exposure to different money environments shifts your sense of what's possible
