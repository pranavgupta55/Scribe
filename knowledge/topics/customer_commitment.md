# Customer Commitment

> Setup fees create sunk cost effect that increases commitment to complete program and reduces churn

## Why It Works
- Customer who paid $5k upfront feels obligated to see results
- Sunk cost effect: more invested in success
- High barrier to exit makes departure feel wasteful

## Implications
- Customer completing program more likely than no-fee cohort
- Results-oriented customers self-select (not everyone can afford $5k upfront)

## Claims
- Big head-long tail principle: high barrier to entry = high barrier to exit; fees increase customer investment and sunk cost — [the_offer_structure_a_stranger_pitched_me_in_a_parking_lot_GMiOc8RKYEo.txt]
- If customer leaves and wants to return, charge setup fee again; sunk cost keeps people committed through completion — [the_offer_structure_a_stranger_pitched_me_in_a_parking_lot_GMiOc8RKYEo.txt]
