# Product Simplification

> Less is more in offer design; each feature should be used, valuable, and defensible against the guilt of unused resources.

## Feature Creep Problem
- Planet Fitness example: offer racquetball, swimming, basketball, weights, cardio; 80% of members only use cardio + light weights
- Result: members feel guilt 'I'm only using 20% of my $59/month subscription, bad deal'
- Same guilt at $29/month; price doesn't change perception if consumption doesn't match offer

## Solution: Trim to Core
- Offer ONLY what customers will use and love; cut everything else
- Each bonus should be worth the price of whole product (e.g., two $12.5K getaways = $25K offer value on their own)
- Simplicity drives simpler marketing, selling, operations, and customer satisfaction

## Bonus Value Strategy
- Pizza night at gym works as acquisition hook (cheaper than membership) but not as retention driver (no one eats it)
- Distinguish: reasons people buy ≠ reasons people stay
- In Couplepreneurs, date night challenges are #1 reason to buy AND stay; everything else is noise
