# Learning Definition and Intelligence

> Intelligence is the speed at which you change behavior in the same condition.

## Framework
- Same condition, new behavior = learning
- Number of iterations to change behavior = measure of intelligence
- Can increase iteration frequency to compensate for fewer iterations needed by others

## How-To
- If you perceive lower intellect, lock in and increase iteration frequency
- Do 7 iterations in one day instead of spread across weeks

## Claims
- Learning is defined as same condition, new behavior - changing your response in an identical situation demonstrates learning. — [how_to_outlearn_everyone_q9qBqnhdWKw.txt]
- Intelligence is speed of learning - a rate that can be accelerated by increasing iteration frequency rather than reducing iteration count. — [how_to_outlearn_everyone_q9qBqnhdWKw.txt]
- Spending 9-to-9 (14 hours daily) in library studying compensated for perceived below-average intellect at Vanderbilt by completing more learning iterations faster. — [how_to_outlearn_everyone_q9qBqnhdWKw.txt]

## Connections
- Intelligence is the speed at which you change behavior in response to new conditions, measured by learning velocity. _(connects to: Intelligence As Speed)_
- Intelligence is how fast you change behavior in the same condition; measured by how many exposures required to learn. _(connects to: Intelligence As Rate)_
- Intelligence equals speed of learning; one person learning in 5 exposures versus 10 is more intelligent. _(connects to: Intelligence As Learning Rate)_
- Both concepts relate to change and condition. _(connects to: Learning Definition)_
- Intelligence is the speed of behavioral change in identical conditions; faster learners apply lessons immediately. _(connects to: Learning Behavior Change)_
