# Event Strategy and Upselling

> Two annual in-person getaway events positioned as marriage insurance with structured meet-and-greets, teaching, and dinner-based closing.

## Event Position
- Frame as 'Couples Insurance' + 'Getaway,' not 'Conference'
- Pitch: 'When you look back, months blend—only spikes are memorable. We help you build those spikes.'
- Hosts stay 2 days post-event to unwind and celebrate their own relationship

## Event Structure (2 Days)
- Day 1: Intro (hook/blowaway) → M1 (meet + specific facts) → M2 → Lunch → Soft Repitch (overcome objections, frame as teaching) → M3 → M4 → Dinner (paid attendees only)
- Day 2: Intro → M1 → M2 → Lunch → M3 → M4 → Wrap (short, 15-30 min objection handling, frame as value add)
- Each Meet & Greet: 1 assigned coach per roster (A/B/C/D teams, color-coded lanyards); coach has 2-3 specific facts about each couple's business/marriage

## Pitch Mechanics
- Informal pitch during 15-min break: 'My team said X, Y, Z from yesterday, so I wanted to handle that up front. It'll help your business too.'
- Close pitch: 'If today was valuable, mind if I tell you more about what we do?' Spend 15 min on offer, call-to-action, close
- Dinner invitation as fast-action bonus (dinner with founders post-event for those who buy)

## Common Mistakes
- Provide too much production value and too little sales intent; weak pitch = only 1 high-ticket close per 50 attendees
- Overthink pitch, add caveats, apologize for selling; simple ask converts 10x better
