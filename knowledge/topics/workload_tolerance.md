# Workload Tolerance

> Repeated exposure to difficulty builds tolerance through lived proof of survival.

## Framework
- Hard thing happens → survive → learn "I can do hard things"
- Next hard thing feels less impossible because precedent exists

## Example
- Training partner wants to hear "you're fine" during hard sets, not "keep going"—signals survival, not performance demand

## Claims
- Inverse PTSD works like workload exposure therapy: surviving hard things teaches you "I've been here before and I didn't die. This is okay." — [you_re_not_winning_you_re_just_getting_used_to_pain_IxqZEmO9WUQ.txt]
