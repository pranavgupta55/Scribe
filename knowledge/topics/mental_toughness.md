# Mental Toughness

> Mental toughness is measurable across four variables: stimulus threshold, behavior-change amplitude, rebound speed, and rebound height.

## Framework
- Threshold: how much bad stimulus before behavior changes
- Amplitude: when behavior does change, how small is the deviation
- Speed: how quickly do you rebound to baseline (resiliency)
- Height: do you rebound to baseline, below, or above (adaptation)

## Key Takeaway
- Stronger mental toughness: longer threshold, smaller change, faster rebound, higher final position
- Trauma = permanent behavior change from aversive stimulus; can be positive if behavioral shift aligns with values
- Life happens for you, not to you when you own your response; power follows blame direction

## Examples
- Mother's death 2025: chose how to show up to family and funeral; built proof of capability for future hardship
- Early hardship is often harder (fewer tools); survival proves resilience for future challenges
