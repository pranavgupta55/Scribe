# Margin Improvement vs Revenue Growth

> In fixed-price-reimbursement models, margin improvement is faster than revenue growth and equally profitable.

## Framework
- Can't raise price (insurance sets it)
- Must reduce COGS: labor efficiency, systems, automation
- 10% margin gain = 67% profit gain

## Why It Works
- Margin work takes 6 months; opening location takes 6 months
- Margin work is lower risk (internal, controllable)
- Margin work generates cash for hiring (no CapEx like new locations)

## Claims
- If you improve margin by 10 percentage points in an insurance-reimbursed business, bottom line increases 67%; opening six more locations takes six months but margin gain is similar effort. — [how_to_say_no_to_the_wrong_opportunities_YJy7PL2apUo.txt]
- Insurance reimbursement rates are fixed (150/hour for dietitians); you can't control price, so you must control COGS and efficiency. — [how_to_say_no_to_the_wrong_opportunities_YJy7PL2apUo.txt]

## Connections
- Expanding before fixing margins creates compounding problems; margin improvement outpaces revenue growth in fixed-price models. _(connects to: Margin Degradation)_
- Margin improvement often faster and equally profitable as top-line growth; shift to bottom-line expansion. _(connects to: Profit Optimization)_
