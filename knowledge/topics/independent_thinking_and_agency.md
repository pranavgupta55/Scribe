# Independent Thinking and Agency

> High agency means deliberately reasoning about every life component rather than adopting pre-canned belief sets from off-the-shelf identities.

## Framework
- Agency: ability to reason from observation for yourself
- Most people pick beliefs off-the-shelf (hipster, redneck, etc.) without questioning each component
- Unique people have deliberately chosen mismatched elements

## Method
- Ask for every decision: What do I want? Does this thing help me get it?
- Question clothes, politics, sports teams, entertainment, career—everything
- Expect discrepancies with peers' choices; that's evidence of thinking

## Key Takeaway
- Short-term cost: rejection from peers who share your old beliefs
- Long-term cost: avoid 10-year confusion about identity and direction
- Unique brand comes from bottom-up reasoning; no one else has your exact life experience

## Examples
- Wore beach sandals for 2 years (closed-toe, gym-pool-beach-restaurant compatible); didn't care if not 'cool'
- Like a movie friends dislike? Say so; don't hold back
- Wore jean shorts, lame shoes—held choice against social pressure
