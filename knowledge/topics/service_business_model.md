# Service Business Model

> 78% of US businesses; slow growth limited by talent supply; high cash flow but people are the bottleneck

## Framework
- Three business stages: attraction (scalable via ads), conversion (requires people for high-ticket), delivery (service personnel)
- Growth is slow and steady; supply constraint is always talent quality and availability
- Talent tiers: low-skill (hard to find reliable people), trades (require experienced technicians), white-collar (require multi-year education)

## Key Numbers
- Scaling decision point: $1-3M revenue is when you decide what business you are really in
- Premium service model: charge for higher-value customers; consistent price raises indicate healthy business with demand > supply
- High cash flow: $100k transaction with $50k cost leaves $50k monthly repeatable profit that can fund lifestyle while scaling

## How-To
- Select one customer avatar and define 1-3 core deliverables (rather than fully bespoke everything)
- Systematize delivery into repeatable steps so training processes can be developed
- Build robust training infrastructure (not weekend training) for onboarding and ramp time; hire smarter raw materials (higher intelligence/horsepower people)
- Create clear career paths (analyst → associate → director) so talent wants to stay rather than leave

## Common Mistakes
- Founder becomes bottleneck by being best technician; leads to founder always solving complex problems, preventing scalable delegation
- Expecting new hires to match founder's skill level immediately; training takes years for white-collar roles, causing management frustration
- Competing on low cost; only works if decided day-one with ruthless operational efficiency (offshore, automation) as core strategy, not reactive pricing
