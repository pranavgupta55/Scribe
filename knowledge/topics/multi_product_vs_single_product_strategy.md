# Multi-Product vs Single-Product Strategy

> Portfolio strategy nearly never works; focus and brand-build one product instead of juggling 4-5.

## Key Takeaway
- 4-5 product portfolio goal sounds good, fails in practice
- Constraint: margins, supply chain, dupe competition, founder bandwidth
- Winner eventually emerges; kill losers, go all-in on brand-building the winner

## Why It Works
- One strong brand > 4 weak brands selling to same customer
- Brand defensibility and PE valuation require focus and depth
- $500M exits need proven brand moat, not arbitrage

## Common Mistakes
- Running 4 mini-businesses cannibalizing same customer base
- Waiting for 9/10 product instead of optimizing existing 6/10 product with brand
- Sunk-cost fallacy: already built/invested, so keep it running

## How-To / Method
- Identify your one clear winner
- Ruthlessly cut the losers (sunk-cost fallacy is a trap)
- Reinvest 100% into brand-building, affiliate recruitment, community

- Patent protection is hard to win at small scale (legal fees drown you); brand-building is a better defensibility strategy than patenting; having the coolest brand and coolest people promoting it makes customers buy even from cheaper knockoffs. — [helping_e_commerce_business_owners_scale_8C_6qojTA78.txt]
- Some of the best money you ever spend is deciding to stop spending bad money; sunk-cost fallacy (already paid for the SaaS build, so finish it) is a trap that destroys capital. — [helping_e_commerce_business_owners_scale_8C_6qojTA78.txt]
- A $500k investment in wholesale inventory at 50% margins returns $150k in profit; the same $500k in a SaaS build ($500k initial, $120k/year maintenance) would likely lose money for 7+ years before potentially breaking even. — [helping_e_commerce_business_owners_scale_8C_6qojTA78.txt]
