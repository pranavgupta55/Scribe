# Brand Measurement & Metrics

> Track influence (behavior change), direction (toward or away), and reach (how many); RPM and revenue metrics outperform vanity metrics like raw views.

## Key Numbers
- Three brand metrics: influence (% who react), direction (toward vs away), reach (number of people)
- RPM (revenue per 1,000 views) reveals quality of audience better than raw views
- 90-day experiment: 2-3x view increase but 50% ad revenue drop = wrong audience

## Framework
- Small, weak, neutral brand = few people recognize it, no behavior change
- Large, strong, positive brand = lots recognize it, behavior changes, majority moves toward you (not polarized)
- Examples: Donald Trump (large, strong, polar); Taylor Swift (large, strong, positive); Apple (large, strong, positive); Mother Teresa (smaller reach, strong, positive)

## Method
- Track real-time leading indicators (ad revenue, email opt-ins, book sales, applications) instead of lagging indicators (12-month deal cycles)
- Use paired metrics to avoid gaming: pair quantity metrics with quality metrics
- For business audiences, RPM correlates with book sales, email opt-ins, and business applications better than raw views
