# Menu Upsell

> Tell customers what they don't need, prescribe what they do, ask A/B preference, enable easy payment

## Framework
- Combine four tactics: unselling, prescription upselling, A/B upselling, card-on-file payment

## How-To / Method
- Unsell: cross out features customer doesn't need; emphasize what remains valuable
- Prescribe: explain how items integrate with prior purchases, personalize use instructions
- A/B: ask preference between two options (flavor, size, time, support type) vs whether to buy
- Card on File: ask 'Want to use the card on file?' rather than asking them to retrieve it

## Key Numbers
- Moved from 0 to 20 supplement sales per day in single session by using A/B + card-on-file tactics

## Why It Works
- Unselling builds goodwill and trust by appearing to game system in customer's favor
- Prescription removes choice paralysis; customers take path of least resistance

## Implications
- The 'Economist' effect: three options at prices $59, $125, $125 (bundle) drives bundle sales vs two-option presentation

- Menu Upsells combine unselling (removing unwanted features), prescription upselling (prescribing what they need), A/B offers (choosing between two options), and card-on-file convenience. — [100m_money_models.txt]
