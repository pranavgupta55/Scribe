# Behavior Prediction

> Find desired behavior in others and immediately reward it; avoid punishing absence.

## Framework
- Responses to absence or wrong behavior trigger avoidance, not correction
- Rewarding presence of desired behavior increases likelihood of repetition

## Example
- Instead of punishing spouse for not talking a certain way (triggers avoidance), reward when they do talk that way (triggers repetition)

- B.F. Skinner demonstrated that free will is an illusion: if you dehydrate a horse, salt its mouth, heat it, and place water one inch away, it will drink without fail — [a_lifetime_of_personal_development_in_18_minutes_PsxOnxIU274.txt]
