# First-Party Data and Iteration

> After learning from models, generate your own data through volume and analyze your own top performers.

## Framework
- Do many reps (batch 1) and identify top 10%
- Find observational differences between top 10% and average
- Create batch 2 with refined approach, rinse and repeat
- Each cycle adds one more inference layer like coats of paint

## Key Numbers
- AI learns from training data with good/bad labels
- Humans learn similarly from volume with feedback loops

## Claims
- First-party data from volume and feedback is more powerful than continuous modeling - after replicating experts, do many reps and analyze your top 10% for insights. — [how_to_outlearn_everyone_q9qBqnhdWKw.txt]
- Observation density increases through iterative coats of paint - each cycle reveals one or two new differences between top and average performers. — [how_to_outlearn_everyone_q9qBqnhdWKw.txt]
