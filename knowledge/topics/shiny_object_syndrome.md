# Shiny Object Syndrome

> New opportunities look perfect because you haven't discovered their hidden problems yet

## Framework
- Old girlfriend: you know all the issues, they feel painful and obvious
- New girlfriend: you only see the exterior, don't know she has crazy ex-boyfriend or other hidden problems
- Your brain defaults to comparing known problems vs unknown problems, favoring the unknown

## Why It Works
- You haven't done enough volume of new model to discover its failure modes
- Licensing might have terrible retention, complex support needs, or other issues not yet evident

## Claims
- Doing both models (licensing and direct service) simultaneously decreases likelihood of hitting goal in either—better to go all-in on primary business or all-in on new business, not split attention — [stop_the_side_hustle_and_grow_the_golden_goose_AhwQYu01rM0.txt]
- New business model looks shiny (no problems discovered yet) while proven model feels worn out (all issues known)—comparison is unfair because you don't know licensing problems until you do enough volume — [stop_the_side_hustle_and_grow_the_golden_goose_AhwQYu01rM0.txt]
