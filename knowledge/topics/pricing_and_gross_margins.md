# Pricing and Gross Margins

> Service businesses require higher margins to absorb talent volatility; SaaS has high margins but low per-unit pricing; E-commerce capital-intensive

## Framework
- Service business pricing: raise prices consistently as demand exceeds supply; metric of founder progress is what they charge (higher price = supply constraint)
- E-commerce margin: must be reinvested into inventory for growth; profit feeds next scaling level; high revenue but low net profit common
- SaaS margin: gross margins extremely high once built, but monetization difficult (enterprise $100k+/year; consumer $9-13/month)

## How-To
- Service business: invest margin increases into recruiting better talent and building brand; margins fund reinvestment in inputs
- Education pricing: split one-time high-value education (big head) from recurring consumable elements (long tail); price according to value provided
- E-commerce: maintain 70-30 brand/direct spending ratio to protect margin; pure arbitrage (buy at $10, sell at $11) has zero margin for brand building

## Implications
- Service business goal: charge premium and serve better avatars; requires good reputation, supply constraint, and track record
- Low-cost service provision: only viable if decided day-one with ruthless efficiency (offshore, automation); cannot pivot without destroying moat
- Premium over volume: $1M per employee in service business is more pleasant than $500k per employee with double headcount
