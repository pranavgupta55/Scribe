# Demand Problem

> Demand problem is demand generation, not offer design; go back to what worked and iterate on distribution.

## Key Takeaway
- 3-week program was converting; ongoing tutoring ads got wrong audience
- Fix audience targeting, not offer structure

## Method
- Re-advertise 3-week summer program (proven winner)
- Create variations of same offer for different times of year
- Ensure targeting reaches parents, not students

## Claims
- $250K annual English tutoring business, 6 tutors, selling high school competitive course prep; want $1M revenue — [how_to_scale_a_tutoring_business_from_250k_to_1m_U8Noy9ly7A4.txt]
- Summer 3-week program was selling well; in February pivoted to ongoing tutoring ads but got 100 leads with wrong demographic (students not parents) — [how_to_scale_a_tutoring_business_from_250k_to_1m_U8Noy9ly7A4.txt]
- Real problem is not offer, it's demand generation; people bought 3-week program, so go back to it as entry point — [how_to_scale_a_tutoring_business_from_250k_to_1m_U8Noy9ly7A4.txt]

## Connections
- Diagnose whether bottleneck is demand generation or capacity; wrong diagnosis triggers wrong tactics. _(connects to: Supply Vs Demand Constraints)_
- Demand generation and supply scarcity are two levers; keeping supply below demand perpetually raises desire and price. _(connects to: Supply And Demand)_
- Demand problems solve via generation and distribution iteration, not offer redesign; constraint is execution, not demand quantity. _(connects to: Demand Unlocking)_
- Businesses confuse operational constraints for demand problems; stopping sales to fix delivery masks the real bottleneck. _(connects to: Supply Constraint)_
- Identify demand vs supply constraint first; demand problem requires distribution iteration, not offer redesign. _(connects to: Business Stage Diagnosis)_
