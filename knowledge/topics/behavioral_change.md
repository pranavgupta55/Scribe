# Behavioral Change

> You control your own intelligence by controlling how often you fail to adapt to conditions.

## Framework
- High intelligence = quick behavior adaptation
- Low intelligence = stuck in old patterns despite repeated conditions

## Implications
- Intelligence is not fixed; it's a skill of adaptation

## Claims
- If you can control your behavior, you can control how intelligent you are; decrease the number of times you fail to change behavior under the same conditions. — [why_watching_podcasts_is_making_you_dumber_bpkxMfNiMbA.txt]

## Connections
- Learning is behavioral change measurable by how you respond in the same condition a second time. _(connects to: Learning Behavior Change)_
- Control intelligence through adaptation to conditions; behavioral change ranges from tiny to massive shifts across fortitude variables. _(connects to: Behavioral Change Scale)_
- Intelligence is controlled via adaptation; fortitude controls behavior depth when tolerance is exceeded. _(connects to: Fortitude Behavior Control)_
- You control intelligence by adapting to conditions; subjective well-being doesn't change despite solving problems. _(connects to: Hedonic Adaptation)_
