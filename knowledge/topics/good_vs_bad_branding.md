# Good vs Bad Branding

> Good branding = pairing that increases sales; bad branding = pairing that decreases sales within your ideal audience.

## Key Takeaway
- Objective measure is money: does pairing increase or decrease sales?
- Majority approval of pairing within ideal audience = good branding
- Majority disapproval within ideal audience = bad branding

## Example
- Dylan Mulvaney + Bud Light: 75% disapproved among core audience = bad branding = lower sales

## Claims
- Dylan Mulvaney and Bud Light was good advertising (let people know about the product) but bad branding (majority of ideal audience disliked the pairing), resulting in lower sales — [what_branding_actually_is_and_how_to_build_one_fast_Vt4D1qgo9ic.txt]
- Bud Light recovered by pairing with Shane Gillis and UFC (man's man associations), and sales began to recover as more of the ideal audience liked the pairing — [what_branding_actually_is_and_how_to_build_one_fast_Vt4D1qgo9ic.txt]
