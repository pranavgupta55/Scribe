# Talent Acquisition and Management

> Hiring better talent at each level unlocks business scaling; there are "levels" of talent quality with discontinuous jumps in capability.

## Framework
- Moving from $1.6M to $5M+ revenue requires hiring talent at $300K/year profit per technician, creating cash flow to sustain multiple specialists
- There are clear levels: $50K employees → $100K → $500K → $1M → multi-million-dollar/year employees; each jump feels like a revelation
- The best talent is always in the future, not behind you; whatever team you have today, better people exist ahead of you

## How-To / Method
- Bump pricing (20-40%) first to create cash flow, then use that margin to hire redundancy—if Tim is absent, another person can fill the role
- For technician roles, run national ads with $50K relocation packages ($25K now, $25K over 6 months) to attract premium talent beyond local market
- Keep W2 employee structure (not 1099 contractor) for workers who have set hours and must follow specific processes—1099 status creates legal risk
