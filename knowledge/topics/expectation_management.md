# Expectation Management

> Set realistic timelines upfront and position the wait as a feature, not a bug.

## How-To / Method
- Commit to a specific 4-month timeline in writing before the sale
- Position early birds as getting the first/best rate
- Remove ambiguity: "You don't have to do anything; just sign here"

## Why It Works
- Customers already have electricity and don't need immediate install
- If incentive is clear (lower rate for patience), they're more likely to stay

## Claims
- Customers cancel not because the service is bad but because they get tired of waiting; expectations are set unrealistically ("next month") and keep getting pushed. — [fixing_a_sales_agency_with_a_40_cancellation_rate_2PfbKVGNgPM.txt]
- Customers may also be switching because cheaper options exist; but if framed correctly (longer timeline for lower rate), they're more willing to commit. — [fixing_a_sales_agency_with_a_40_cancellation_rate_2PfbKVGNgPM.txt]
