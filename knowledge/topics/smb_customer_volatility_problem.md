# SMB Customer Volatility Problem

> SMBs suck at business, so their bad months = your churn; pricing must protect you from their volatility

## Why It Works
- SMBs run on emotion and volatility, not systems
- When their customers dry up, they cut services they view as discretionary
- You inherit their business instability directly

## Framework
- Either: price so low they don't churn (grind model, limits profit)
- Or: price so high only healthy businesses can afford you (removes SMBs from portfolio)
