# Education and Info-Media Business

> Fastest initial scaling (zero to revenue quickly) but hard ceiling at $1-3M; retention is nearly zero as graduates leave by design

## Framework
- Business shape: steep rapid growth then hard stop; fast, easy, cheap to start creates many competitors
- Fundamental problem: teaching people creates competition because graduates can immediately teach what they learned
- Value proposition: skill moving someone from $40k to $100k annually ($60k/year increase) has massive appeal to learners

## Key Numbers
- $10 trillion global education market because skills and tools drive per-capita GDP growth
- Scaling ceiling: most education businesses plateau at $1-3M revenue; growth beyond requires promotional skill many founders lack
- Retention metric: education business retention is nearly zero by design (goal is graduation, not retention)

## How-To
- Split pricing: high price for one-time valuable core education (big head); lower recurring price for consumable elements (long tail: community, updated creative)
- Add stickiness through discount buying power (collective purchasing on materials at 50%+ discount), continued education updates, and community access
- Quality control: don't dilute with mediocre coaches; better to teach 10 people well than 100 people poorly; smaller audience with premium results retains reputation

## Examples
- Harvard calculus costs $200k; CCBC costs $2k; price gap driven by brand association reducing perceived risk, not content difference
- Cordon Bleu culinary school brand confers job placement advantage; graduates more likely to land jobs because school's reputation for quality
- Continued education model: doctors pay $250k to become doctors but only $3k annually for license maintenance credits; pricing must reflect value being provided
