# Churn Problem

> Biggest issue is churn; activation is low, not because product is bad but because advisors don't like sales

## Key Takeaway
- Churn is typical for agencies; solution is better avatar targeting + better ops, not a product problem

## Key Numbers
- Close rate: ~10%, 1 account/month from 170 referral partners

## Claims
- Financial advisors can't close deals; main business constraint is churn, not lead generation — [he_wants_3m_but_he_s_the_bottleneck_EbcqyxLLWN4.txt]

## Connections
- Churn is the biggest bottleneck; 80% churn means constant treadmill; switch to stickier models or fix activation to stay solvent. _(connects to: Churn Vs Recurring Revenue)_
- Agencies churn because they don't use the service, not quality; lack of usage in pricing leaves money on table. _(connects to: Agency Churn Root Cause)_
- Separating skill training (one-time) from consumables (recurring) prevents high-churn misalignment; low activation reveals sales aversion. _(connects to: Churn Reduction In Subscription Models)_
- Trial abandonment inflates total churn; measuring trial-to-customer and paid-customer churn separately reveals true retention gaps. _(connects to: Churn Root Cause Analysis)_
- Low activation within 10 days predicts churn; product quality matters less than onboarding friction. _(connects to: Trial To Activation Problem)_
- 40-50% annual churn is hidden constraint; sticky products should be <20%; churn prevents growth. _(connects to: Churn As Growth Ceiling)_
