# Premium Pricing

> Higher prices increase perceived value, emotional investment, and client commitment, resulting in better results and stronger business fundamentals.

## Virtuous Cycle of Price
- Raised price → increased emotional investment → perceived higher value
- Higher investment → client takes action → better results achieved
- Better results → attracts higher-quality clients → easier to serve
- Increased margins → invest in excellence → deliver more value

## Why Higher Prices Work
- Higher price signals quality and scarcity (wine taste test effect)
- Increases skin-in-the-game commitment to follow through
- Attracts self-selecting motivated buyers who believe in value
- Allows investment in systems, people, and customer experience

## Common Mistakes
- Competing on price with commodities (race to bottom)
- Assuming lower prices attract more customers (often inverse)
- Cutting prices to close sales (teaches negotiability)
- Confusing revenue growth with profit growth
