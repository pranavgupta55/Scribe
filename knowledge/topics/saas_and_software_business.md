# SaaS and Software Business

> Slowest initial growth, highest required capital, but infinite scale and stickiest potential revenue of all models

## Framework
- Business shape: slow costly development (1-2 years, millions capital) before revenue; then explosive growth post-product-market-fit
- Infinite scale means low per-unit cost but also low willingness to pay; Netflix pays $100M+ for shows to charge $10/month; enterprise pays $100k/year despite massive IT budgets
- Founders often quit early due to emotional toll of months/years of negative cash flow watching bank account decline

## Key Numbers
- Gross margins: incredibly high once product-market-fit; but monetization is difficult (consumer: $9-13/month; enterprise: hundreds of thousands annually but small % of customer spend)
- Volume requirements: to make real money with low per-customer pricing, need 100M+ customers; exponentially harder than incremental scaling
- SMB/Enterprise SaaS more profitable: customers willing to spend more, higher switching costs, more defensible

## How-To / Method
- Obsess over product quality and customer feedback; but don't build everything customers request or create Frankenstein product
- Iterate rapidly on core user flows: Dropbox founder tested users uploading files and initially 5/5 failed, requiring weeks of iteration per flow
- Focus on retention over acquisition; growth looks like users activate and never churn (horizontal line), not exponential curves with high churn
- Reduce complexity through exceptional product design; elegance comes from iteration and deep thinking, not feature bloat

## Common Mistakes
- Vibe-coded software has not yet produced validated $100M+ businesses; struggles with robustness vs. human-built software, though improving
- Feature bloat and complexity kill user experience; many features require exceptional design to avoid reducing usability
- Over-focus on acquisition before retention is solved; growth from retention compounds; growth from churn + new signups does not
