# Market Expansion & Growth Tailwinds

> Shrinking markets are uphill battles; growing industries (20%+ CAGR) enable growth by default even with weak execution

## Key Takeaway
- Growth is easier than distribution; pick expanding markets over declining ones

## Key Numbers
- Alternative education CAGR: 20%+ annually
- Declining: formal education (-6% yearly), newspapers, tobacco

## Examples
- Growing: energy, AI, healthcare, cybersecurity, e-commerce, alternative education
- Shrinking: newspapers, formal education, tobacco, brick-and-mortar retail

## Claims
- Growing markets (energy, AI, healthcare, cybersecurity, e-commerce, alternative education at 20%+ CAGR) are fundamentally easier than shrinking ones like newspapers and formal education — [what_makes_the_perfect_business_5_things_3fsJFUvA6Ts.txt]

## Connections
- Local markets cap growth; expand radius via branding and premium positioning; shrinking markets are uphill battles versus growing ones. _(connects to: Market Expansion And Geographic Scaling)_
- Growing markets enable growth via weak execution; shifting focus to bottom-line expansion creates more value than top-line. _(connects to: Profit Optimization)_
- Conquer local markets before expansion; growing industries enable growth by default even with weak execution. _(connects to: Local Market Domination Before Expansion)_
- Growing industries enable growth by default; growing pie of existing market beats entering new markets. _(connects to: Market Share Vs New Markets)_
