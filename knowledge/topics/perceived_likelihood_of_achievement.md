# Perceived Likelihood of Achievement

> Customers buy outcomes, not time; one-on-one delivery increases perceived likelihood of success compared to scalable alternatives.

## Framework
- Outcome = what customer really wants (e.g., lose weight)
- Vehicle = how you deliver (one-on-one vs. PDF)
- Likelihood of achievement = perceived chance they'll succeed
- Ease = how difficult the path feels
- Reputation = implied guarantee of competence + intention

## Why It Works
- Same outcome, different vehicle = vastly different perceived success
- One-on-one daily call > PDF diet plan for weight loss perception
- Your reputation acts as insurance; they believe you'll deliver
