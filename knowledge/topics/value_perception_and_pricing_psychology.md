# Value Perception and Pricing Psychology

> Rich customers evaluate price vs. value (ROI); poor customers only see price tag; rich people shop high-to-low; pricing communicates quality and value to market.

## Framework
- Rich person: sees $20K as question of ROI ('Is this worth $20K?'); evaluates value first
- Poor person: sees $20K as 'expensive'; evaluates cost first
- Pricing is two-way communication: you tell market what you're about; they self-select accordingly

## Key Takeaway
- Wealthy customers shop price high-to-low (premium section first) because they want better stuff and skip lines
- Poor customers shop price low-to-high to minimize spend
- In general, higher priced = better quality perception; people use price as value proxy

## How-To / Method
- Before saying price, anchor: 'It's super expensive.' Rich person mentally preps for high number they expect; poor person braces for shock; both are anchored fairly
- Writing price on paper and sliding it (vs. saying it aloud) reduces choking on the number

## Caveats
- Pricing too low ($99 for health/doctor service) can backfire—people don't believe it works if it's cheap; raised price 50% increased close rates
