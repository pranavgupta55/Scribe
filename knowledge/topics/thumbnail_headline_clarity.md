# Thumbnail & Headline Clarity

> Clear thumbnails and headlines that exactly match content increase click-through rates and reduce bounce rates; curiosity-gap strategies underperform for educational content.

## Framework
- Left thumbnail (vague): looks like 'a rule related to money' but unclear
- Right thumbnail (clear): obviously shows a map/blueprint from zero to a million
- Clear version gets more clicks because people know what they're getting

## Why It Works
- When people click on something they don't know about, lower percentage get what they want
- When you say 'this is what this is about' and deliver exactly that, people trust you more
- Higher click-through rates on accurate descriptions than curiosity-gap approaches

## Common Mistake
- Following entertainment marketing advice about not telling everything upfront
- Entertainment audiences tolerate curiosity gaps; education audiences want clarity
- This was a hard lesson for Alex to learn but high-impact for results
