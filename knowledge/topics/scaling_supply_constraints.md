# Scaling Supply Constraints

> Four levers solve supply constraints: price, delivery ratio, productization, and hiring. Choose based on team bandwidth, market position, and X-factor defensibility.

## Four Solutions
- Raise price: fewer buyers, more revenue per person (limited if market resistance high)
- Change ratio: 1-on-1 to 1-on-4 to 1-to-many (preserves X-factor, increases capacity)
- Productize: high-touch → self-paced, templates, modules (easier to scale but dilutes personalization)
- Hire: delegate delivery to trained coaches (requires infrastructure, training rigor, higher payroll)

## For Couplepreneurs: 1-on-4 Chosen
- Price increase ruled out: already struggling to sell $25K; need 3-4x increase to justify 1-on-1 cap
- Productization ruled out: already tried ($5K tier); couldn't sell at same price point as high-touch
- Hiring ruled out: couple cash-constrained, prefer to stay boutique
- 1-on-4 ratio: solves supply, maintains 80% of X-factor, requires minimal infrastructure change

## Why Hiring Fails for Coaches
- X-factor (personality, skills, experience) hard to replicate in weekend training
- Tier-1 replacement coaches cost $100K+/year salary; economics break at $25K per-customer price point
- Result: mediocre coaches hired cheaply, customer experience tanks, business reputation declines

## Claims
- Supply constraint solutions include: (1) raise price/fewer buyers, (2) change delivery ratio (1-on-1 to 1-on-many), (3) productize, (4) hire people.
