# Perceived Likelihood

> Conviction that the offer will work for the specific buyer; increased by social proof, guarantees, and demonstrated track record.

## Why It Matters
- People pay dramatically more for 10,000th patient than first, even identical service
- Surgeon with track record more valuable than untested competitor
- Track record eliminates uncertainty and justifies premium pricing
- Same service, same results, different perceived likelihood = vastly different prices

## How-To / Method
- Communicate through social proof (testimonials, case studies, results)
- Share third-party edifications and certifications
- Provide guarantees that demonstrate confidence in results
- Use specific metrics and track record data in sales messaging
