# Rich Mindset vs. Poor Mindset Pricing

> Rich evaluate offers by cost-to-value ratio; poor evaluate by absolute cost; this drives drastically different buying behavior.

## Framework
- Poor mindset: price $20K → 'that's expensive' → says no
- Rich mindset: price $20K → 'for what?' → evaluates the value
- Berkshire Class A at $20K: incredibly cheap (normally $800K); Lamborghini at $20K: steal of century

## Why It Matters
- Same price triggers different responses based on buyer mindset
- Rich buyers care about ratio, not absolute dollars
- This explains why high prices can increase perceived value

## Claims
- Rich people think in cost-to-value ratio; poor people think in absolute cost. Rich person says 'for what?' when given a price; poor person says 'that's expensive' — [how_i_made_my_first_high_ticket_sale_B7P5WCVfnPE.txt]
