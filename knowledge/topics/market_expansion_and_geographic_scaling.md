# Market Expansion and Geographic Scaling

> Local markets cap growth; expand radius via long-term brand building, premium pricing, and geographic expansion

## Key Takeaway
- Local market is finite; if you don't expand, you must dominate market you're in
- Thought leadership expands radius; people travel to brands they trust (e.g., Amen Clinic, national reputation)

## Framework
- Path 1 (local domination): ads + content in single market, premium pricing from reputation
- Path 2 (geographic expansion): become national brand via content, then scale via franchising/multi-location

## Examples
- Chiropractor at $2.4M: dominate local with ads + content, or expand to new markets with proven playbook

## Connections
- Local markets cap growth; expand radius via branding and premium positioning; shrinking markets are uphill battles versus growing ones. _(connects to: Market Expansion Growth Tailwinds)_
- Founder-heavy location has margins; split attention and geographic expansion require brand premium and superior margins to overcome coordination costs. _(connects to: Location Expansion Dynamics)_
- Geographic expansion requires owner business time; can't scale locations if owner is delivering 80% of work. _(connects to: Multiple Location Expansion)_
- Franchising and geographic expansion both scale to 100+ locations by using other people's capital and brand. _(connects to: Franchising Strategy)_
