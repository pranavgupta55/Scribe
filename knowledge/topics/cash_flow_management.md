# Cash Flow Management

> E-commerce requires careful forecasting and partner coordination; service business generates high cash flow enabling lifestyle business

## Framework
- E-commerce cash trap: despite growing revenue, profits reinvested into inventory; cash available for owner decreases as business scales
- Example: $10 profit per unit × 10 units = $100/month profit; 10 new customers want to buy next day but only $100 profit available to buy 10 more units; growth capped by free cashflow
- With 12-week manufacturing lead times, must forecast demand 12 weeks out; prediction errors cause stockouts (lost revenue) or excess inventory (tied-up capital)

## Key Numbers
- Service business: $100k transaction with $50k cost = $50k monthly repeatable profit; enables founder to take salary while scaling
- E-commerce: $250M acquisition portfolio means recurring revenue reinvestment, not founder extraction; different cash profile than revenue size suggests

## How-To
- E-commerce: develop robust cashflow forecasting based on inventory cycles and lead times; plan capital requirements 3+ months ahead
- Secure lending partnerships for growth phases; capital constraint can kill business despite good fundamentals if unable to finance inventory growth
- Service business: can maintain lifestyle while scaling because cashflow is positive; use for owner salary and talent recruitment investment
