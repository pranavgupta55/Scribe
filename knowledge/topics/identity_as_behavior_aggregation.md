# Identity as Behavior Aggregation

> Who you are = the list of things you do, not some separate essence

## Framework
- Identity = aggregation of all behaviors
- Describing 'who someone is' = describing 'what they do'
- Example: 'Chris does podcasting' is part of 'who Chris is'—there's no difference between doing and being

## Claims
- Identity is the aggregation of all behaviors you do; when you describe who someone is, you describe what they do (e.g., 'he is a carpenter' means 'he does carpentry') — [why_you_shouldn_t_put_your_actions_on_a_pedestal_70LSQk_RH_M.txt]
