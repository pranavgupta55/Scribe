# Gratification Timing

> Third Marshmallow Fallacy: delaying gratification forever wastes resources; learn when to reap, not just sow; risk-adjust future outcomes.

## Framework
- Early career: longer delay = bigger outcomes (dose relationship)
- Later career: must learn when to accept gratification
- Winners get reinforced for delaying more and more; harder to stop delaying

## Key Takeaway
- Not all delayed outcomes are worth delaying; risk-adjust
- $100M today > uncertain $big-amount 10 years away (5 years to use resources)
- It's okay to make money; it's okay to just make money

## Examples
- Personal training client: $4K/month in cash; not scalable, not high-multiple; enabled survival during gym launch
- Content creator with 1M followers: built audience, never asked for anything; must learn when to monetize

## Implications
- Invest vs. consume must be in the middle somewhere, not extremes
- Forever sowers die with unused resources; must draw personal line of when to reap
