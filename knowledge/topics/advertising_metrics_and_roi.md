# Advertising Metrics and ROI

> Financial frameworks measuring advertising efficiency and profitability across methods

## Framework
- LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit): total money customer spends minus all fulfillment costs = available budget for acquisition + business operations
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): total spend (payroll, ads, software) ÷ customers acquired
- Ratio: LTGP ÷ CAC; 3:1 is minimum, 5:1+ is excellent

## Key Numbers
- At 4:1 ratio on $5M revenue: can handle 4 customers per dollar of CAC; improves to 4.7:1 within 12-24 months at Acquisition.com portfolio companies
- If CAC > 3x industry average: advertising problem; if CAC < 3x average but ratio poor: sales/product problem

## Claims
- The 3:1 LTGP-to-CAC ratio (lifetime gross profit to customer acquisition cost) is the minimum threshold for profitable scaling; above 3:1 focus on increasing LTGP over decreasing CAC — [100m_leads.txt]
