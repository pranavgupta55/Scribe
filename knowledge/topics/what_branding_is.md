# What Branding Is

> Branding is deliberate pairing of things through outcomes—not feelings or logos, but strategic associations that drive behavior.

## Framework
- Branding fundamentally is pairing: your thing + customer action + outcome they get from it
- Brand exists only in the association between elements; scatter the flowers and the brand disappears
- Brands happen everywhere all the time, but good branding is deliberate
- A brand isn't what you say it is; it's what customers associate with it through repeated pairings

## Examples
- Coca-Cola pairs product with yum outcome; Harley pairs with loyalty outcomes; Apple pairs with waiting-in-line outcomes
- Ranching: cattle brands created associations with ownership, affecting how people treated the animals
- One rotten flower (bad pairing) ruins the entire bouquet (brand perception)

## Key Takeaway
- The purpose of branding is to make money by changing customer behavior in your favor when they see your brand
- This concept carries independent of audience size; even parents have high-influence brands with their children
