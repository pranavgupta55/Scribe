# Intelligence as Rate

> Intelligence is how fast you change behavior when exposed to new conditions.

## Key Numbers
- Person A learns in 1 exposure, Person B in 5 exposures → Person A is more intelligent (higher learning rate)

## Framework
- Intelligence = inverse of exposures needed to change behavior
- Fewer reps to learn = higher intelligence

## Claims
- Intelligence is the rate of learning—how many times do you need exposure to the same condition before you change behavior? Fewer exposures = higher intelligence. — [why_watching_podcasts_is_making_you_dumber_bpkxMfNiMbA.txt]

- Intelligence is how fast you change behavior when exposed to new conditions, not innate capacity. _(connects to: Intelligence As Speed)_
- Intelligence is learning velocity; person requiring 5 examples versus 10 is more intelligent in that context. _(connects to: Intelligence As Learning Rate)_
