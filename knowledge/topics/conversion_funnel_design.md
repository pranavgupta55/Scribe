# Conversion Funnel Design

> Test 4-hour date night as alternative to 5-day challenge; same information density, higher attendance, natural pitch opportunity.

## Hypothesis
- Information required for purchase decision: ~5 hours total
- Spreading over 5 days = low continuity, high drop-off, memory decay
- One 5-hour session = state flow, continuity, top-of-mind buying intent

## Date Night Framing
- Opening: 'If you're anything like us, date night is not business or fun—it's both. We're doing that together.'
- Timing: Thursday or Friday evening (day irrelevant per testing); 4-hour window natural for couples activity
- Closes with informal pitch: 'If today resonated, do you mind if I tell you a bit more about what we do?' (5-15 min)

## Expected Outcome
- 2x attendees vs. 5-day (511 reg → 26 atnd = 5% show-up; date night 511 reg → 52 atnd = 10%+ expected)
- Same or higher close rate due to extended in-person time reducing buyer anxiety

## Claims
- Test the '4-hour date night' event instead of 5-day challenge because: (1) same information density fits in one session, (2) customers experience state flow/continuity, (3) date night positioning attracts 2x attendees, (4) permits natural pitch at end without disruption. — [if_i_wanted_to_scale_a_service_business_in_2026_here_s_what_N5MExtki_VI.txt]
