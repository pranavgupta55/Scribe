# Feedback Loops & Reinforcement

> What happens AFTER behavior determines if it repeats; behavior repeats because it has served you in past.

## Framework
- Reinforcement = what happens after behavior
- More reinforcement = more repetition
- Less reinforcement = extinction
- Behavior persists because it was once rewarded

## Examples
- Get angry at wife → she stops crying → anger gets reinforced (you 'win')
- Yell at one employee late → everyone else fears you → communication slows (unintended consequence)
- Be present for upset person → they thank you → new reinforcer strengthens
- Manager's fear of getting punished slows communication upward

## Why It Works
- Explains why behavior persists even when harmful long-term
- Shows how to change behavior: shift the reinforcer
