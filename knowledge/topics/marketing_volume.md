# Marketing Volume

> Scaling marketing is doing violent amounts of work consistently; volume compounds when input and output scale together.

## Framework
- Paid ads: more creative → more spend → more platforms
- Each step reduces risk; creative has lowest downside
- Content/outbound: same logic applies

## Key Numbers
- 4 sales/month current = need 7x advertising minimum to 28 sales/month
- Could need 14x if efficiency drops as you scale

## Key Takeaway
- More is not just money; it's creative volume, consistency, and platform expansion
- Volume requires manpower; budget for production staff not just media spend

## Claims
- For paid ads, scale in reverse order of risk: more creative first, then more spend, then more platforms — [you_need_to_advertise_more_lBNpcvKvf38.txt]
- If you're doing 1 sale per week scattered across days, scale to 7 sales per week requires 7x or 14x advertising volume (assuming efficiency drop) — [you_need_to_advertise_more_lBNpcvKvf38.txt]

## Connections
- Demand for creative explodes; volume is the moat; scaling marketing means violent amounts of work with consistent input-output compounding. _(connects to: Creative Volume Demand)_
- Scaling marketing via volume compounds wins, but performance marketing channels create a vicious spiral that destroys margins. _(connects to: Performance Marketing Doom Loop)_
- Consistent violent volume compounds; saturation comes through testing keyword markets which have far more capacity than founders assume. _(connects to: Scaling Media Buying And Keywords)_
- Violent consistent volume compounds; mass market needs capital and extreme efficiency while high-end is more forgiving. _(connects to: Volume Vs Margins Tradeoff)_
- Volume compounds when input and output scale together; most people do 1-10% of required volume. _(connects to: Volume Underestimation)_
- Scaling volume matters, but absolute profit matters more; high conversion rate beats high volume at low margin. _(connects to: Profit Vs Volume)_
- VC-scaled businesses often sacrifice unit economics and service quality for growth volume, losing to smaller profitable competitors long-term. _(connects to: Venture Backed Scaling Illusion)_
- Three-step scaling framework: increase volume, improve efficiency, then test new platforms once core channel is optimized. _(connects to: More Better New Scaling)_
