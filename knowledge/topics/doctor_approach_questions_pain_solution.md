# Doctor Approach (Questions, Pain, Solution)

> Three-Step Framework for Selling AI Services

## Key Takeaway
- Positioning a pain involves asking questions (e.g., 'What do you think happens when...?'), presenting a solution (e.g., 'We can install this AI...'), and closing by emphasizing value and price.

## Evidence & Examples
- An example of positioning a pain is comparing a client's limited Google reviews (5) to a competitor's 150 reviews, highlighting their own shortcomings.

## How-To / Method
- The three steps are: 1) ask questions to position a pain, 2) present a solution (e.g., AI installation), 3) close by emphasizing value and price, which clients assess independently.

## Why It Works
- Closing strategies work because clients decide based on their own assessment of value and price, not just the provider's.

## Implications
- Practitioners should apply the three-step framework to position pain, demonstrate value, and guide decision-making in AI sales.
