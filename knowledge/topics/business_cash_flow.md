# Business Cash Flow

> At $1-3M revenue, limited cash flow forces you to learn everything. After $3M, you can hire specialists.

## Framework
- $1M revenue × 30% margin = $300K profit
- Subtract living expenses (assume $100K) = $200K available
- Can hire one person, not a team
- You must learn sales, marketing, operations

## Claims
- At $1-3M, cash flow is $200K-600K annually; can afford one hire but not fully built team—must learn everything yourself — [pick_one_sales_channel_or_stay_broke_Yjt_YfzF1kQ.txt]
- Until $3M, you fund growth from profit. Budget: 30% margins on $1M = $300K profit. Subtract living expenses, little left for hiring — [pick_one_sales_channel_or_stay_broke_Yjt_YfzF1kQ.txt]

## Connections
- At $1-3M revenue, limited cash flow forces owner to learn everything; low margins trap you between overwork and hiring risk. _(connects to: Business Swamp Constraints)_
- Both revenue milestones and business model choices (service vs. e-commerce) determine cash flow generation and talent hiring capacity. _(connects to: Cash Flow Management)_
