# Revenue Retention & Stickiness

> Most critical advantage; 100%+ net retention means compounding growth even without new customer acquisition

## Framework
- Logo retention: percentage of customers retained (max 100%)
- Net revenue retention: revenue from same cohort today vs past year (can exceed 100%)
- Structural churn: involuntary (customer dies, business closes, moves away)
- Voluntary churn: avoidable, caused by poor product or service

## Key Numbers
- Month 1: 20%+ churn (highest)
- Month 3: 10% drop-off
- Month 6: final critical milestone
- After month 6: 2% monthly churn stabilizes

## How-To / Method
- Create tiered pricing ($9/month to $99/month) to allow upgrade path
- Qualify customers at entry to ensure fit
- Focus heavily on first 30 days experience
- Drive customers to month 3 and month 6 milestones

## Claims
- Revenue retention is the most critical business metric; without it, you remain purely a sales business rather than a growth business — [what_makes_the_perfect_business_5_things_3fsJFUvA6Ts.txt]
- Net revenue retention above 100% means even losing some customers, remaining customers increase spend enough to offset churn and enable business growth — [what_makes_the_perfect_business_5_things_3fsJFUvA6Ts.txt]
- Sticky businesses like term life insurance (pay until death) and alarm systems (low switching) scale easily; non-sticky businesses like roofing and car sales are one-time transactions — [what_makes_the_perfect_business_5_things_3fsJFUvA6Ts.txt]
- Business with low retention should focus on fixing retention first before adding other advantages; if industry has zero retention, switching to one with retention may be strategically important early in career — [what_makes_the_perfect_business_5_things_3fsJFUvA6Ts.txt]
