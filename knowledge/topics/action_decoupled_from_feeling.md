# Action Decoupled from Feeling

> You don't need to feel confident to take action; the action comes first, confidence comes after.

## Key Takeaway
- Feeling inadequate doesn't prevent outcomes
- Action is independent of emotion

## Why It Works
- World responds to what you do, not what you feel
- Results build confidence retroactively

## Claims
- Realizing you can feel not good enough but still do the stuff required to get the outcome is a breakthrough: the feeling and the result are separate. — [you_don_t_have_to_feel_good_enough_to_win_HwTWXXoVm-8.txt]
- Realizing you could "get away with" taking action while feeling inadequate was freeing; you could just do the thing and the result would come anyway. — [you_don_t_have_to_feel_good_enough_to_win_HwTWXXoVm-8.txt]

## Connections
- Confidence follows action, not vice versa; separate feeling from behavior to execute despite doubt. _(connects to: Feelings Vs Actions)_
- Core maturity skill: action comes first, confidence after; break link between emotion and behavior. _(connects to: Separation Of Feeling And Action)_
- Action comes before confidence; courage is doing despite fear while cowardice is not doing despite wanting to. _(connects to: Courage And Fear)_
