# E-Commerce Business Model

> Capital-intensive model with fast growth until distribution or supply chain hits limits

## Framework
- Business shape: quick growth until hitting breaking points (cash, traffic, supply chain, distribution)
- Growth blocked by inventory constraints, manufacturer capacity limits, logistics bottlenecks, or distribution partnerships maxing out
- Cash must be reinvested into inventory for continued growth; profit bootstraps next scaling level

## Key Numbers
- Perfect e-commerce company spends 70% on top-of-funnel brand, 30% on direct purchase
- Repurchase rates of 60-70% from first purchase cohort indicate strong product
- Most startups spend 100% on direct-purchase only, creating commoditized, non-defensible businesses

## How-To
- Build or own manufacturing with proprietary ingredients/techniques creating defensible moat
- Establish lean performance marketing team combined with brand-first approach
- Invest in strong logistics (pick-pack-ship) and consider decentralized manufacturing in key markets
- Develop exceptional email marketing backend with attribution, messaging flows, and campaign optimization

## Common Mistakes
- Ignoring brand building and competing purely on arbitrage (smash-and-grab) invites immediate competition
- Failing to manage cashflow forecasting with long lead times (12+ weeks) leads to stockouts
- Underestimating 3PL/logistics switching costs; as you scale beyond a partner's capacity, you must add redundant partners
