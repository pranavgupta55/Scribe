# Capital Requirements for Scale

> Mass-market scaling requires capital; high-end is bootstrappable.

## Example
- Tesla nearly bankrupt multiple times achieving mass-market Model 3 production
- High-margin Roadster was the sustainable starting point

## Claims
- To serve the poor masses at low prices requires tons of money, automation, and time; Tesla has nearly gone bankrupt multiple times trying to achieve this. — [why_you_should_charge_5x_more_not_50_more_CTJRsVA89Oc.txt]

## Connections
- Mass market requires capital and extreme efficiency; high-end is bootstrappable and forgiving. _(connects to: Volume Vs Margins Tradeoff)_
- Mass-market scaling requires capital; high-end is bootstrappable; perfect economics before scaling prevents broken models compounding. _(connects to: Scaling Discipline)_
- Mass-market scaling requires capital whereas high-end is bootstrappable; start at your operational load level rather than overextending. _(connects to: Operational Scalability)_
- High-end is bootstrappable via one-on-one at 10X; 100% margin funds aggressive growth without capital. _(connects to: Bootstrap Scaling)_
- Don't let capital constraint prevent growth; high-end is bootstrappable while mass-market needs external capital. _(connects to: Capital Allocation)_
