# Buy X Get Y Free

> Raise base price and bundle free items to create compelling offers while preserving profit margins

## How-To / Method
- Calculate: Buy 3 shirts for $10 each = $30 OR Buy 1 shirt for $30 get 2 free (same math, better perception)
- Give more free than paid for maximum appeal (Buy 2 Get 10 Free beats Buy 10 Get 2 Free)
- Free items can differ from paid items; cheaper free items can be more effective than expensive ones
- For services, structure as 'Buy 6 months Get 6 months Free' or similar duration combinations

## Key Numbers
- Boot Factory example: triple price for single pair, add 2 free pairs, attract bachelorette groups reliably

## Why It Works
- Free drives more interest than discount messaging
- Raises customer lifetime value through front-loaded purchases

## Common Mistakes
- Not raising base prices before implementing offer; must preserve profit margins
- Making offer to more than 10% existing customer base can destabilize cash flow
- Not having continued offers for prepaid customers

## Claims
- Buy X Get Y Free reframes discounts as free items; offering 'Buy 1 Get 2 Free' generates more interest than a 33% discount on the same products. — [100m_money_models.txt]

## Connections
- Both Bonuses and Buy X Get Y Free reframe pricing as bundle value: Bonuses stack additional items to expand price-to-value discrepancy, while Buy X Get Y Free reframes discounts as free items. _(connects to: Bonuses)_
