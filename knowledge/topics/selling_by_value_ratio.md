# Selling by Value Ratio

> Always frame price against value delivered, not against other prices; rich buyers evaluate the ratio, not the number.

## Examples
- Berkshire Class A at $20K when normally $800K: deal of the century
- Lamborghini at $20K: steal (normally way more)
- Same $20K, completely different reactions based on expected value

## Framework
- Never lead with absolute price
- Lead with value: 'Here's what you get,' then price makes sense
- Rich people automatically calculate ratio; poor people just see the number
