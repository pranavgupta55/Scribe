# Self-Awareness and Constraint Identification

> Maniacally focus your effort on your one constraint; be self-aware enough to identify it correctly and willing to witness your own consistency.

## Framework
- Self-awareness: identifying your constraint and focusing violently on solving it through multiple simultaneous approaches (read many books, hire an expert, consume all videos)
- Commitment and focus are sister twins—the elimination of alternatives

## Key Numbers
- Ramosi read 15 books in 2 weeks on family office structures when learning that domain
- You can't witness consistency unless you're also consistent (e.g., can't see someone gym-ing at 4 AM unless you're there too)

## Common Mistakes
- Most entrepreneurs haven't properly identified their constraint OR take insufficient action to solve it (or both)
- Don't understand the volume required; if you knew, you wouldn't begin because the goalpost seems unfathomable
