# Eliminating Secondary Business Lines

> Kill businesses that consume time without delivering core value or margins.

## Key Takeaway
- Airbnb management: 60 properties live, requires constant team expansion, founder hates it
- Partner deal: client does arbitrage, founder gets 1/3 revenue = $100K/year for ongoing CEO role (bad deal)
- Cleaning company: built accidentally; now operating in too many directions

## Why It Fails
- When management fails, clients call the founder; he owns the relationship even if partner does 80% of work
- Marginal revenue is far too low relative to time; better to focus on high-margin acquisition
- Dilutes focus from the core business that's actually scaling
