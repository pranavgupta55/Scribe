# Psychological Principles in Sales

> Shame lives in darkness; reframe group delivery as healing, not embarrassing. Distinguish shame (breaking others' rules) from guilt (breaking your own rules).

## Shame vs. Guilt Definition
- Shame: breaking others' rules you respect
- Guilt: breaking your own rules
- Fear of group disclosure = fear of being embarrassed = belief that you're alone in your struggle

## Group Reframe for Couples Coaching
- Counter-belief: couples problems are universal (relationships, business challenges, time, communication) not unique
- Examples: church couple circles, AA meetings, Christian groups where intimate disclosures are normal
- Position: 'You're not alone; shame only lives in darkness. Bringing it to light shows it's normal.'
- One-on-group ≠ less personalization; equals better outcomes because clients hear others' solutions too

## Decision-Making Autonomy
- Unlimited daily access trains outsourcing of decisions; quarterly strategy teaches self-reliance and prioritization
- Coaching value is on 3-5 big life decisions/year, not day-to-day questions
- Frame: 'We're here for the rare decisions with biggest impact, not daily tactics. ChatGPT can handle day-to-day.'
- Offering emergency lifelines (2-4/year) provides safety valve without creating dependency
