# Sales Team Structure and Compensation

> Inbound and outbound sales are separate career paths with different compensation; inbound pays lower per-deal but offers reliability; outbound is high-variance high-reward.

## Framework
- Outbound sales (door-knocking, cold calls) has high variance: bad day = zero sales, good day = 7 sales; stressful, requires high compensation
- Inbound sales with qualified leads has lower variance: bad day = 2 sales, good day = 7 sales; more reliable, families prefer it
- Only proven outbound savages graduate to inbound leads; mixing teams causes outbound performers to abandon street work for easier inbound

## How-To / Method
- Separate teams: keep 'savages' on outbound; select one best performer for inbound; hire new inbound sales specialist; pay lower commission on inbound due to lead cost
- Commission structure should differ: inbound commission lower because you're paying for leads; outbound can be higher per deal

## Examples
- Roofing company: door-knockers remain on streets generating own leads (high risk/reward); one savage closes inbound leads from paid channels (lower stress, more reliable)
