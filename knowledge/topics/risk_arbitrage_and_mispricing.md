# Risk Arbitrage and Mispricing

> People misprice risk by fearing losses more than valuing gains; smarter entrepreneurs exploit this gap.

## Key Takeaway
- Humans overestimate downside and underestimate upside
- Most people take too little risk because they're afraid
- Real wealth comes from identifying mispriced bets

## Examples
- Howard Marks made billions buying distressed debt others perceived as too risky
- Elon Musk having two successful companies suggests he understands risk differently
