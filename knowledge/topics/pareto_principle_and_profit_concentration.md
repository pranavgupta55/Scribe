# Pareto Principle and Profit Concentration

> 20% of customers create 80% of profit; within that 20%, 4% create 64% of profit; profit distribution is far more extreme than wealth distribution.

## Framework
- 20% of customers = 80% of company profit
- Within that 80% of profit, 4% of customers = 64% of the profit (power law repeats)
- Within that 64%, top 1% = 51% of that segment; one customer often generates more profit than bottom 100

## Why It Works
- Serving one premium customer at premium price often doesn't cost much more than serving 99 lower-tier customers; fixed overhead covers both
- Premium customer profit margin typically 10:1 or higher vs. base tier; $8K profit on one vs. $3 profit on each of many

## Implications
- Scaling profitable businesses means focusing on top-tier customers, not growing customer count
- If you want to do less and make more, the path is serving fewer people at higher prices—not scaling to masses
