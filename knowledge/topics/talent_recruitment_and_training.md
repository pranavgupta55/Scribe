# Talent Recruitment and Training

> Service businesses bottlenecked by talent supply; must invest in recruiting smarter people and robust training systems

## Key Takeaway
- Service business constraint is always talent: supply of good people << demand; better people are harder to find and cost more
- Founder cannot expect to train someone in weekend to replicate 10-year skill mastery; this expectation leads to mediocre delivery and reputation damage
- Higher-tier roles (white-collar, professional services) require multi-year apprenticeship (analyst → associate → director) before independent work

## How-To
- Invest recruiting budget to attract highest-quality raw materials (smarter, higher-intelligence people) who ramp faster and deliver better
- Build robust training infrastructure and career paths so talent wants to stay; clear advancement (analyst → associate → director) creates retention and motivation
- Productize your knowledge and systematize delivery so training is teachable; this reduces founder bottleneck and compounds talent quality

## Examples
- Management consulting hiring (Ivy League backgrounds, entrepreneur experience) creates higher-quality delivery and better talent stickiness
- Career path clarity allows talent to see advancement opportunity; without it, hard-to-find people leave for competitors or start own firms
- Fitness coaching easier to hire (passionate people do it for free) but harder to retain customers; trades/white-collar harder to hire but customers stick because they don't want to learn
