# Churn Reduction

> Higher one-time setup fees correlate directly with lower churn through sunk cost and commitment mechanisms

## Framework
- Barrier to exit increases with barrier to entry
- Sunk cost psychology: customer invested $5k, less likely to quit than customer who paid $19

## Key Numbers
- $100 fee: near-zero churn
- $19 fee: significantly higher churn
- $5,000 fee: 2+ year client lifespan (vs 3-4 month norm)

## Implications
- Higher fees = longer customer lifetime = higher lifetime value
- Fee acts as quality filter: commits serious customers, deters browsers
