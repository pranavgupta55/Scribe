# Paid Advertising Fundamentals

> One-to-many advertising to cold audiences via platform spending (Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc.) to reach many strangers simultaneously

## Key Numbers
- Phase 1 (Track): Set up conversion tracking before spending dollar one
- Phase 2 (Lose Money): Budget 2x first-month customer profit to test; shut down before 1x spend if no leads
- Phase 3 (Print Money): Reverse-engineer spend from customer goal; pad 20% for efficiency degradation

## Framework
- Three ad components: Call-out (notice), Value elements (interest), CTA (action)
- Three phases: Track money → Lose money to learn → Print money at scale

## How-To / Method
- Step 1: Pick platform where audience exists and you understand ad formats
- Step 2: Target lookalike audience from best customers; add interest/demographic filters
- Step 3: Create ad with strong call-out (labels, yes-questions, if-then, ridiculous results), value elements (What-Who-When), and clear CTA
- Step 4: Drive to landing page matching ad visually/verbally; collect contact info
- Step 5: Measure LTGP vs. CAC; maintain 3:1 ratio minimum for profitability

## Examples
- One gym challenged: 'I'm looking for 5 Chino Hills residents to take part in a free 6-week challenge' → 19 sales from $1,000 spend
- Facebook data showed top 0.1% of advertisers test 11x more creative than average

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing advertising problem (low response) with sales problem (low conversion)
- Not budgeting long enough to find winner; need 2-3 months per platform to break even

## Connections
- Paid advertising reaches cold audiences; close rates spike when targeting only people with actual buying power. _(connects to: Target Audience Qualification)_
- Paid advertising reaches cold audiences at scale; TikTok monetization is poor, real revenue from Google/Meta ads. _(connects to: Platform Monetization)_
