# Iteration Vectors

> Two ways to compete: reduce iterations needed OR compress iterations tighter.

## Framework
- Vector 1: Iteration count - how many trials needed
- Vector 2: Iteration velocity - how fast you do the trials
- You control velocity even if iteration count is higher

## Examples
- Peer: 3 iterations over 3 weeks
- You: 10 iterations in 1 week (locked in, no distractions)
- You win on timeline despite higher iteration count

## Claims
- If someone takes 3 iterations to learn and you take 10, you can still beat them by compressing 10 iterations into one day instead of stretching them over weeks. — [you_think_you_re_not_smart_enough_REDPMH2BMrY.txt]
- You can either reduce iterations needed (inherent ability) or increase iteration velocity (work ethic and focus) to overcome intelligence gaps. — [you_think_you_re_not_smart_enough_REDPMH2BMrY.txt]
