# Revenue Retention and Stickiness

> Critical differentiator for business value; determines whether growth compounds or requires constant new customer acquisition

## Framework
- E-commerce: 60-70% repurchase rate from first purchase cohort signals strong product and word-of-mouth potential
- SaaS: >100% net revenue retention (NRR) from original customers compounds value; <60% indicates no stickiness and unsustainable business
- Education: structure as one-time big-value purchase + recurring consumable elements (community, updates) to create revenue retention where none naturally exists

## Key Numbers
- Facebook growth model: activation with zero churn (retention) compounds more than exponential growth curves with high churn
- SaaS scaling rule: if you retain X% of customers forever, filling the front end with new users creates exponential growth because base compounds
- Product quality compounds: first book success determines attendance at subsequent launches; first book failure kills momentum for sequel launches

## How-To
- Build exceptional products so customers want to buy repeatedly and refer others; second sale bigger than first requires first was great
- SaaS: integrate deeply into customer workflow so switching costs are high; focus on metrics like daily/weekly active usage, not just signup counts
- Education: balance one-time education value pricing against recurring consumable elements; don't charge full education price for low-value community access
