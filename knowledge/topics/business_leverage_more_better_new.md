# Business Leverage: More, Better, New

> Bigger, working businesses should do more of the same (not new); new changes risk destroying alignment; highest leverage is solving why you can't do more.

## Framework
- 6 candies randomly stacked = almost impossible to align by chance
- Working business = rare aligned stack; changing one variable risks breaking alignment
- Bigger stack = more variables = higher likelihood change breaks it

## Method
- More: do more of what works (highest risk-adjusted move)
- Better: tiny variation on what works
- New: least likely to work, most resource-intensive
- Allocation: one new thing per year maximum for mature companies

## Key Numbers
- AB test control won 36/36 splits; likelihood of new variant improving it = low
- Tiny conversion percentages mean you need clarity, not cleverness

## Examples
- Gym Launch: 30-40M plateau 3 years; brand-building took years before revenue growth kicked in
- Gym Launch: turned 11/11 directors in 12 months; culture felt worse at 6 months but correct course; didn't change direction

## Caveats
- Real risk: second/third/fourth most profitable actions instead of most viable
- Business owners solve artificial future problems, destroying real businesses
- Constraint problems take 18 months; worse at 6 months; don't change course
