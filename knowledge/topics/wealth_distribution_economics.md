# Wealth Distribution Economics

> Top 1% holds more wealth than bottom 90% combined; where you sell dictates profitability and all businesses should chase the money concentration.

## Key Numbers
- Top 10% earn 40% of U.S. income
- U.S. household net worth: $163 trillion total
- On $100 wealth distribution: bottom 50% = $2, next 40% = $28, next 9% = $38, top 1% = $32 (more than bottom 90% combined)

## Implications
- 69% of aggregate wealth is in just top 10 people (out of 100 percentiles); competing for bottom $2 against 50 businesses is inefficient
- If you're selling to people who don't have money, you cannot make money; must target wealth concentration
- Rich people have more spending power and fewer spending constraints; same product is affordable vs. unaffordable based on who buys it
