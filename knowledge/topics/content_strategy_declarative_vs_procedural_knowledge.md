# Content Strategy: Declarative vs. Procedural Knowledge

> Teach declarative knowledge (about something) to sell; reserve procedural knowledge (how to do something) for the paid product.

## Framework
- Declarative knowledge: teaches concepts, frameworks, why things work—gives immediate value just by exposure
- Procedural knowledge: teaches tactics/steps—requires independent execution to extract value, delaying purchase

## Example
- Declarative: 'Crossing $2M to $5M raises valuation multiple from 3x to 7x, making $4M business worth $35M' (no how-to, creates belief)
- Not: 'Here's how to set up a domain, warm it, integrate Klaviyo' (procedural, audience walks away saying 'I'm good')

## How-To / Method
- Teach how it works conceptually, break old vs. new model beliefs, then sell the implementation
- 99% of people won't buy but judge you on quality of free content
