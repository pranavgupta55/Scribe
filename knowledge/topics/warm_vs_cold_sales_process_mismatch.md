# Warm vs. Cold Sales Process Mismatch

> Applied warm sales playbook (simple, conversational) to cold leads (skeptical, need more context); cold process works for both, warm only for warm

## Framework
- Warm prospects: already know/like you, need light touch
- Cold prospects: don't know you, need full credibility build
- Warm process on cold = under-convert
- Cold process on warm = over-convert (no issue)

## Example
- Book launch audience (warm): simple pitch works, but even better with process
- Cold email prospect: needs full USP, proof, offer explanation

## Claims
- $6M revenue from warm/inbound word-of-mouth with high close rate; now booking 100 cold calls/month but closing only 6% (50% of warm rate) — [booking_100_calls_a_month_but_closing_half_Tk0e0z8h64Y.txt]
- Built for warm, it only works on warm; built for cold, it works everywhere—cold sales process is superset, warm is subset — [booking_100_calls_a_month_but_closing_half_Tk0e0z8h64Y.txt]

## Connections
- Warm leads skipped full process lose close rate; cold process works for both warm and cold. _(connects to: Warm Lead Underestimation Problem)_
- Warm and cold sales require different processes; applying warm to cold leads fails because cold audiences need proof. _(connects to: Warm Process Mistake)_
- Cold traffic requires different process than warm; applying warm to cold fails without proof and context. _(connects to: Sales Motion Changes With Cold Traffic)_
- Cold process works for both cold and warm leads; warm process fails on cold leads because they need more context and skepticism handling. _(connects to: Cold Process On Warm Leads)_
- Cold email teaches universal skills; cold process works for both cold and warm when executed properly. _(connects to: Beginner Sales Skill Development)_
