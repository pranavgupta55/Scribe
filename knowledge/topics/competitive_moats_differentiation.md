# Competitive Moats & Differentiation

> Sustainable competitive advantages come from brand, capital requirements, specialized skills, patents, network effects, or proprietary processes

## Framework
- Capital moat: barriers based on CapEx (power plants, semiconductor fabs)
- Skill moat: specialized knowledge competitors lack (NVIDIA, nuclear engineering)
- Patent moat: defensible IP requiring novelty, non-obviousness, and usefulness

## Examples
- NVIDIA: high CapEx + semiconductor engineering skills = moat
- Trade secrets: recipes, processes, specialized sauce only founder knows

## Why It Works
- Moat reduces competitive pricing pressure
- Network effects (more users = more value) create natural stickiness
- Incumbents have difficulty replicating if moat is defensible
