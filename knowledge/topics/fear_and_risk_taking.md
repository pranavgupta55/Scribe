# Fear and Risk-Taking

> Fear dissolves when crystallized into specific, detailed worst-case scenarios; most imagined catastrophes are survivable and unlikely.

## Framework
- Write down worst-case scenario in excruciating detail, step-by-step
- Realize it's not as bad when crisp vs. when hazy
- Others care far less about your failures than you think (everyone is self-focused)

## Key Takeaway
- Dare greatly because nothing you own is permanent; you're renting everything
- Commitment must be to effort, not outcome; control what you can, roll the dice on the rest
- No great achievement comes from fear of others' opinions
