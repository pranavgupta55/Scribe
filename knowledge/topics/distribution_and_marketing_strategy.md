# Distribution and Marketing Strategy

> Determines customer acquisition rate and, combined with product quality, drives long-term business growth

## Framework
- Best marketers find best products because they can sell volume; prioritize 10th sale (retention/quality) over first sale (initial conversion)
- Product quality compounds through marketing: great first product → successful first launch → audience for second product; bad first product → no audience for sequels
- Media/content distribution: at scale, can go from zero to $100M revenue in 60 hours if product, offer, history, and audience align perfectly

## Examples
- Book launch case: first book success creates audience for leads book launch; leads success creates audience for money models launch; each depends on prior book quality
- E-commerce optimal: 70% top-of-funnel brand-awareness spending, 30% direct-to-purchase; builds associations and reduces commoditization risk
- Service business end-state: demand exceeds supply so much that marketing becomes secondary; reputation and word-of-mouth drive growth

## Implications
- Invest heavily in product quality first; great product + great marketing = exponential growth; bad product + great marketing = rapid reputation destruction
- For information/education business: real-world proof (exits, results, business success) is most valuable distribution asset; credentials matter less than demonstrated mastery
