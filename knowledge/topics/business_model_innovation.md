# Business Model Innovation

> Recurring revenue models (maintenance contracts, SaaS) command higher valuations than project-based; elevator maintenance is higher-margin, more valuable than construction.

## Key Numbers
- Elevator business year one: $3M revenue, 30% profit margin = $900K profit
- Construction company: $11M revenue, 18% profit margin = $1.98M profit
- Elevator scales faster, allows cleaner exit, better recurring model

## Framework
- Recurring revenue (maintenance contracts) is easier to sell, exit, and value than project-based construction
- Commercial construction: value based on backlog + assets; recurring elevator: value based on subscription base + renewal rates

## Implications
- If founder can grow elevator $3M → $6M in one quarter, that's $3M new annual value; if construction exit is $5M, elevator focus is opportunity positive
